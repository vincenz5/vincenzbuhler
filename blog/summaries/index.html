Summaries

Money

Spankbank
  • People are not good at coordinating.
  • Allows people to apply to join a guild.
  • Loot tokens are for LPs. These are held by the Moloch contract.
  • The funds are held in the Guild Bank contract.
  • Each proposal has an applicant and list of token addresses that are being distributed, amount of tokens, and voting shares requested.
  • https://github.com/SpankChain/spankbank
MakerDAO's Dai
  • Stabletoken collateralized on-chain with Ether

Lending

Dharma
  • Lend Dai, Ether, or USDC on-chain through smart contracts.
  • Interest rates of 2.50%, 14.00%, etc.
  • Borrower has to overcollateralize the loan, so in case of non-payment, collateral is given to lender.
Salt Lending
  • Similar to Dharma but more traditional
Tally Coin
  • Allows people to contribute to projects using Bitcoin.
CoinLoan
  • Also similar to Dharma or Salt Lending
Compund
  • Money markets on-chain
ETHLend
  • Lending platform on Ethereum.

Scaling

POA Network
  • Proof of Autonomy sidechain for stabletoken transactions.
  • https://forum.poa.network/t/proof-of-autonomy/1743
xDai
  • Tool for using xDai sidechain.
  • Allows for mobile wallet usage without any passwords or mobile wallet apps.
  • Transactions are essentially free and very fast.
Counterfactual
  • State channels for off-chain transactions secured by on-chain code.
Loom Network
  • Sidechains based on Plasma Cash.
  • Plasmachain: DPos protocol
  • Protocol backed by Loom token.
L4
  • Research on state channels.
